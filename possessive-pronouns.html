Possessive Pronouns

Pronouns are words that replaces nouns. Possessive pronouns are words that can replace a possessive adjective and a noun at the same time.

This jacket is my jacket. → This jacket is mine.

Your dress is beautiful, but I prefer her dress. → Your dress is beautiful, but I prefer hers.

This is not your key. Yours is over there.

We have a different possessive pronoun for each type of subject:

Subject Possessive Adjective Possessive Pronoun
I My Mine
You (singular) Your Yours
He His His
She Her Hers
It Its Its*
We Our Ours
You (plural) Your Yours
They Their Theirs

*used only with "own".

It's important to know the difference between possessive adjectives and possessive pronouns. You will need to memorize them, but don't try to memorize the words in isolation. It's easier to read a lot of sentences that use them and then try to reproduce those sentences.

More examples

Your house is big. Mine isn't.

The blue car is mine and the red one is hers.

I like your t-shirt.

It's my problem, not yours.

Attention: It's important to read the examples and questions aloud. This will help you with memorization.
