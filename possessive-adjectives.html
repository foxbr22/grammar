Possessive Adjectives

In English, there are three main ways to talk about possession. Here you are going to learn about possessive adjectives.

Possessive adjectives are words used before a noun to demonstrate possession.

Possessive Adjectives

This is my book.

That's her pen.

Possessive adjectives are ALWAYS followed by a noun.

Possessive Adjectives Table

Subject Possessive Adjective Example
I my This is my book.
You your Is that your car?
He his He forgot his keys.
She her She loves her job.
It its The cat licked its paw.
We our We cleaned our room.
They their They finished their homework.

Things to be careful with

Possessive Adjectives (blue) can be similar to possessive pronouns (red):

  • her book (possessive adjective) vs hers (possessive pronoun)
  • his book (possessive adjective) vs his (possessive pronoun)

Their use, however, is different. So be careful. Possessive adjectives need a noun next to them, while possessive pronouns don't.

Don't worry. You will learn the difference through practice and it will become natural. You won't have to think about it all the time.
